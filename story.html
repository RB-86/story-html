Dorothy Gale, a young orphaned girl living in Kansas with her Uncle Henry and Aunt Em, is swept away to the magical land of Oz when a cyclone carries her house away. The house lands on the Wicked Witch of the East, freeing the Munchkins from her tyranny and making Dorothy a hero in their eyes. Dorothy is told by the Good Witch of the North that the only way she can return home is to seek out the powerful Wizard of Oz in the Emerald City. Along her journey to the city, she meets three companions: the Scarecrow who wants a brain, the Tin Woodman who desires a heart, and the Cowardly Lion who seeks courage. Together, they face numerous challenges including the Wicked Witch of the West who wants revenge for her sister's death. After many trials and tribulations, including a journey through a dark forest and encounters with creatures like Kalidahs and Winged Monkeys, they finally reach the Emerald City. The Wizard agrees to help them if they bring him the Wicked Witch's broomstick. With bravery and teamwork, they defeat the Witch and return to the Wizard with the broomstick, only to discover he is just an ordinary man who came to Oz by accident. However, he still grants the Scarecrow, the Tin Woodman, and the Cowardly Lion their wishes: a diploma, a heart-shaped watch, and a potion of courage respectively. Ultimately, the Good Witch of the South helps Dorothy return home with the magic silver shoes (ruby slippers in the 1939 film adaptation). She clicks her heels together three times while saying "There's no place like home," and wakes up back in Kansas, surrounded by her family and friends. "The Wonderful Wizard of Oz" is a timeless tale of friendship, courage, and the journey to discover that what one seeks might have been within them all along.
